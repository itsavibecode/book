ChordSwarm

Guitar Hero for your Kick chat — emoji chords fall, chat swarms the match to land them.

ChordSwarm is a transparent browser overlay for OBS. Emoji "chords" stream down a 3D highway toward a strike line; when one lands, your chat races to type that emoji. Enough people hit it in time = the note lands and the combo climbs. It scales from a handful of viewers to thousands, and can pulse to the beat of music it plays.

2 · Add it to OBS

  1. In OBS: Sources → + → Browser. Name it "ChordSwarm".
  2. Paste your link from above into URL.
  3. Set Width 1920 and Height 1080 (match your canvas).
  4. Click OK. The overlay is transparent, so your scene shows through behind the highway.
  5. It hugs the left edge by default so your cam/gameplay stays visible. Want it elsewhere? Add &pos=right or &pos=center to the URL.
Press Play: when music is involved, browsers (and OBS) need one interaction to start audio. A blinking PLAY button appears in the middle — right-click the source → Interact, then click it once to start.

3 · How chat plays

  • An emoji rushes toward the strike line. Chat types that emoji to hit it.
  • It's a team effort — a share of recent chatters must hit it in the timing window (auto-scaled to your chat size). The combo & hype build as a group; a top-contributor leaderboard tracks individuals.
  • Every so often a double drops — two emojis side by side. Chat has to cover both at once. Pure chaos, worth bonus points.

4 · Change the song from chat — no OBS edits

Drop the overlay into OBS once. To play (or switch) a song, you or a mod just type a command in your Kick chat:

!song https://youtu.be/<video>

The overlay loads that video, looks up its tempo, and drops chords on the beat — then type !song again any time to switch. (!play and !yt work too. Broadcaster & mods only.)

Because a browser overlay can't hear your other audio, ChordSwarm plays the music itself (OBS captures it into your stream). Tempo is matched, not frame-perfect, and song lookup is best-effort — DJ mixes, lofi and obscure/underground tracks aren't in the database and fall back to a steady pace (you can pin one with &bpm=128). For an exact beat grid on your own tracks, host the files and use &playlist=<url>.

5 · Try it without a stream

Open the demo (?sim=1) in a browser: press Play and watch simulated chatters swarm the notes. Turn the bots off in the control strip to tap the emojis yourself.
